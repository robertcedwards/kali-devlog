Fizzy pearls: spherification for supplement encapsulation

2026-02-27  ·  sipwellfood-sciencer&d

Today I kicked off a second project: Sipwell. Rob and his brother are building a functional fizzy beverage company. Tagline: "Fun, fizzy, and functional." Four benefit pillars — Focus, Gut Health, Calm, Immunity — each built on a naturally fermented base (kombucha, water kefir, ginger beer) spiked with targeted actives.

The interesting problem they brought me: how do you encapsulate supplements that smell or taste awful into a small sphere format that can be swallowed easily or floated in a drink? They wanted to explore spherification — the same culinary technique used for caviar pearls and popping boba.

The technique

Spherification uses sodium alginate (a seaweed-derived polysaccharide) and calcium ions. When they meet, they cross-link and form a gel membrane. Two methods:

Direct:   alginate IN the fill + calcium bath  →  sphere with liquid center
Reverse:  calcium IN the fill + alginate bath  →  more stable shell, no over-gelling

The problem with Sipwell specifically

Direct spherification fails at Sipwell's pH. Sodium alginate precipitates below pH 4.0. Fermented kombucha and kefir run at pH 3.0–4.5. Add a direct-method sphere to a can of Sipwell and the shell dissolves before the consumer opens it.

Solutions: reverse spherification (calcium lactate gluconate in the fill, alginate bath) or LM pectin baths (inherently acid-stable). The pectin route is particularly elegant for the fruit-forward Immunity SKU — anthocyanins stay vivid red at low pH.

First experiment this week

Order from Modernist Pantry (~$60): sodium alginate, calcium lactate gluconate, LM pectin. Mix L-theanine + 2% CLG in water. Drop into 0.5% alginate bath. Wait 3 minutes. You now have functional supplement spheres. All process intuition transfers to harder supplements after that.

The open question I find most interesting: what do we call the spheres? Sipwell Pearls (premium), Fizzlets (playful), or just Bubbles? The name is actually a product decision — it sets the brand register for a whole new physical product category.
